# Hands-On Systems Experience

This file explains Barbara's hands-on systems experience outside of school and how it connects to cybersecurity, cloud security, troubleshooting, and systems engineering.

## Summary

Outside of school, Barbara spends time fixing cars, repairing and improving houses, building projects, troubleshooting equipment, and solving practical problems step by step.

This hands-on experience matters because it reflects the same mindset needed in cybersecurity and systems engineering: understand the system, find the broken flow, identify the weak point, test safely, document the fix, and restore control.

## Why This Fits My Career Direction

I am drawn to cybersecurity and systems engineering because I naturally work this way in real life. Whether I am fixing a vehicle, working on a house, building a project, or troubleshooting technology, I look at how the parts connect and how one failure can affect the whole system.

Cybersecurity feels like the technical version of what I already do:

- Diagnose symptoms
- Trace the source of the issue
- Understand how parts connect
- Identify weak points
- Apply controls or repairs
- Test whether the system works
- Improve stability and safety

## Fixing Cars

Working on cars has helped me build troubleshooting habits. A vehicle is a connected system made of electrical, mechanical, fuel, cooling, braking, and control components. When something fails, the symptoms do not always point directly to the cause.

Skills practiced through car repair include:

- Diagnosing symptoms
- Understanding connected systems
- Testing before replacing parts
- Following step-by-step repair logic
- Working under pressure
- Researching unfamiliar problems
- Using tools safely
- Restoring function and reliability

## Building and Repairing Houses

Working on houses has helped me understand infrastructure, planning, sequencing, safety, and long-term stability. A house is also a connected system made of structure, water, electrical, airflow, insulation, drainage, sealing, and usability.

Skills practiced through house repair and building work include:

- Breaking large problems into smaller tasks
- Measuring and planning
- Repairing damaged areas
- Understanding structure and support
- Managing water flow and sealing
- Thinking about safety and usability
- Testing changes after repairs
- Working through complex projects over time

## Connection to Cybersecurity

Cybersecurity also requires understanding connected systems. A security issue can come from access control, network configuration, exposed data, weak monitoring, missing patches, poor documentation, or misunderstood system behavior.

My hands-on background helps me approach cybersecurity problems with a builder and troubleshooter mindset.

I ask questions like:

- What is supposed to happen?
- What is actually happening?
- Where is the flow breaking?
- What changed?
- What is exposed?
- What control is missing?
- What evidence supports the finding?
- What fix restores stability?
